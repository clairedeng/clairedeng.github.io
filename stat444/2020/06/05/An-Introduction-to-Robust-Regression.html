Power transformation and the bump rule

A positive variable y can be transformed using a power transformation indexed by $\alpha$: A scatter-plot of $(x_u, y_u)$ for $u\in P$ may be “straightened” bby applying (possibly) different power transforms to each coordinate to give a new scatter-plot of the re-expresses data $(T_{\alpha_x}(x_u), T_{\alpha_y}(y_u))$.

Because each of the coordinates has its own power transformation, there will be two different ladders of transformation: the x ladder and the y ladder.

The bump rule does not always work.
