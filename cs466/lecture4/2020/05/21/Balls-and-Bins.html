Balls and Bins

21 May 2020 » CS466, Lecture4

We have $m$ balls and $n$ bins. Each ball is thrown to a uniformly random bin independently. We would like to study the distribution of the balls into the bins.

Expected Number of Balls in a Bin

Let $B_{i, j}$ be the indicator variable that ball $j$ is in bin $i$.

The $E[\text{# of balls in bin } i] = E[\sum_{j=1}^m B_{i,j}] = \sum_{j = 1}^mE[B_{i, j}] = \sum_{j = 1}^m Pr(\text{ball $j$ in bin $i$}) = \sum_{j=1}^m \frac1n = \frac mn$.

When $m = n$, the expected number of balls in a bin is $1$.

Expected number of empty bins

Let $Y_i$ be the indicator variable that bin $i$ is empty.

Then, $E[Y_i] = Pr(\text{bin $i$ is empty}) = (1-\frac1n)^m \approx e^{-\frac mn}$using $1-x \le e^{-x}$ and $1-x\approx e^{-x}$ for small $x$.

So, $E[\text{# of empty bins}] = E[\sum_{i=1}^n Y_i] = \sum_{i=1}^nE[Y_i] = ne^{-\frac mn}$.

When $m = n$, we expected to see a $\frac 1e n$ bins are empty.

Maximum Load - What is the maximum number of balls in a bin?

A simpler question - what $m$ do we expect to see two balls in a bin (a collision occurs)?

The probability that there are no collision in the first $m$ balls is: $$ \begin{align*}

Pr(\text{no collision in first $m$ balls}) & = (1-\frac1n)(1-\frac2n)…(1-\frac{m-1}{n})
&\le e^{-\frac1n}e^{-\frac2n}…e^{-\frac{m-1}{n}}
&= e^{-\frac{m(m-1)}{2n}}
&\approx e^{-\frac{m^2}{2n}}

\end{align*} $$

The probability would be smaller than $1/2$ when $m \ge \sqrt{2n\ln2}$.

For $n = 365$, when $m \ge 22.49$, the probability that the maximum load is at least two is at least $\frac12$. This estimate is very close to the exact number.

We expect to see a collision when $m = \theta(\sqrt n)$.

The maximum load when $m = n$

The probability that a bin has at least $k$ balls is at most ${n\choose k}(\frac1n)^k < (\frac{ne}{k})^k(\frac{1}{n})^k = \frac{e^k}{k^k}$.

Some useful bounds are: $(\frac nk)^k< {n \choose k} < \frac{n^k}{k!} < (\frac{ne}{k})^k$.

Coupon Collector - What m do we expect to have no empty bins?

Let $X$ be the number of balls thrown until there are no empty bins.

Let $X_i$ be the number of balls thrown until there are exactly $i$ empty bins.

Then $E[X] = E[\sum_{i=1}^n X_i] = \sum_{i=1}^n E[X_i]$.

Each $X_i$ is a geometric random variable where $Pr(X_i = k) = (1-p)^{k-1}p$ with $p = \frac in$

In words, $X_i$ is the number of trials until the first success, when the success probability is $p$.

$E[X_i] = \frac1p$

$E[X] = \sum_{i=1}^n E[X_i] = \sum_{i=1}^n \frac ni \approx n\ln n $ ($\sum_{i=1}^n \frac1i = H_n$, the n-th harmonic number).
